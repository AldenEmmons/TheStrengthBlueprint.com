# Our Lingo

> The specific words, phrases, and terminology we use.
> Claude should default to this language — not generic fitness industry language.

## Signature Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| "Pain Is Not A Life Sentence" | Core tagline — hope + authority |
| "Pain ≠ Tissue Damage" | Pain doesn't mean something is broken |
| "Fear-avoidance is controlling you" | The mental loop keeping people stuck |
| "Root cause, not symptom" | We go deeper than surface-level fixes |
| "Consistency matters more than exercise type" | Takes pressure off perfect programming |
| "Pain up to 3–5/10 is acceptable if it returns to baseline within 24 hours" | The load tolerance rule |

## Key Terms

- **PNE** — Pain Neuroscience Education. The education phase that rewires how clients think about pain.
- **Graded Exposure** — Progressive reintroduction to movement and load to overcome fear-avoidance.
- **Proximal-to-Distal Loading** — Building core/hip stability before targeting the injured area.
- **Motor Control** — Movement quality and neuromuscular patterning before adding strength load.
- **Kinesiophobia** — Fear of movement. A key thing we address in Phase 1.
- **Fear-Avoidance** — The behavioral pattern where avoiding movement makes pain worse over time.
- **Cortical Inhibition** — Muscle shutting down due to pain signals — isometrics help reverse this.
- **Baseline** — The client's normal pain level. We use it as a reference point for load tolerance.

## The Three Phases (Always Use These Names)

- **Phase 1 — Foundation**
- **Phase 2 — Strengthen**
- **Phase 3 — Perform**

## Tone Rules

- Direct and confrontational when appropriate — we challenge what clients have been told
- Authoritative but not cold — we understand their frustration
- Specific over vague — cite numbers, criteria, mechanisms
- Never say "just rest it" or imply pain means stop
