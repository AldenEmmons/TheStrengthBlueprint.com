# Training Theology

> The core belief system behind how The Strength Blueprint trains people.
> Everything we build — programs, content, copy — must reflect this.

## Central Premise

**Pain Is Not A Life Sentence.**

Built on evidence from 40+ meta-analyses. We reject conventional wisdom that tells people to rest injuries or accept chronic pain as permanent.

## Core Beliefs

- Pain ≠ Tissue Damage
- Fear-avoidance is controlling you — movement avoidance perpetuates pain more than tissue damage itself
- Root cause, not symptom
- Consistency matters more than exercise type
- Evidence-based, clinical-grade methodology — not bro science

## The Three-Phase System

**Phase 1 — Foundation**
Pain education, isometrics for the affected area, and core stability work while inflammation settles.
Proximal stability first. Motor control before load.

**Phase 2 — Strengthen**
Progressive loading once motor control is established.
Movement progresses: bilateral → unilateral, partial ROM → full ROM.
Pain up to 3–5/10 is acceptable if it returns to baseline within 24 hours.

**Phase 3 — Perform**
Sport-specific or goal-driven programming with objective progression criteria.

## Key Methods

- **Pain Neuroscience Education (PNE)** — 100–400 minutes of education over weeks to address pain beliefs and kinesiophobia
- **Graded Exposure** — Structured progression from modified movements to full loading to overcome fear-avoidance
- **Proximal-to-Distal Loading** — Core stability before addressing the injured area
- **Motor Control Before Strength** — Movement quality precedes progressive overload
