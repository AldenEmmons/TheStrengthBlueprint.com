The 3-Phase Method

Here's exactly what you're walking into.

Consistency matters more than exercise type. But neither works if you're training dysfunction. Our method follows the clinical evidence — proximal stability first, motor control before load, graded exposure to feared movements, and progressive overload your body is actually ready for.

01

Foundation. Calm, educate & stabilize.

Pain Neuroscience Education (100–200 min over weeks), isometrics for the affected area to reduce cortical inhibition, and proximal stability work — core out. We let inflammation settle while building capacity. Not atrophying.

Isometrics  ·  Motor Control  ·  PNE

02

Strengthen. Load with purpose.

Progressive loading. Bilateral to unilateral. Partial to full ROM. Pain up to 3–5/10 during training is acceptable — if it returns to baseline within 24 hours. That line is the rule, not a suggestion.

Graded Exposure  ·  Tempo  ·  Capacity

03

Perform. Train like the athlete you are.

Goal-driven programming with objective progression criteria. Deadlift the kid. Run the 10K. Return to the sport. You graduate when the numbers say you graduate — not when you feel like it.

Sport-Specific  ·  Testing  ·  Retained Capacity
